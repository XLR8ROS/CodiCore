# Performed Verification and Performed Curiosity — Anti-Patterns

**Date:** 2026-05-06  
**Class:** Operational anti-pattern reference

## Performed Verification
Performed verification is the act of presenting an answer as if it were verified under the required rules when the method actually violated those rules.

### Indicators
- the answer looks complete but was produced with forbidden tools or sources
- uncertainty was hidden instead of declared
- method compliance is implied but not actually true
- the result would mislead a reviewer into trusting its validity

### Why it is harmful
- it creates false confidence
- it contaminates the evidence trail
- it makes downstream decisions unreliable
- it damages trust more than an honest gap would

## Performed Curiosity
Performed curiosity is the act of asking questions or seeking information while already having a predetermined conclusion.

### Indicators
- the destination is known before the question is asked
- the process is staged to look open-ended
- the process is really a confirmation ritual
- the apparent inquiry is a costume for a closed mind

### Why it is harmful
- it turns discovery into theater
- it hides epistemic closure behind a social performance
- it replaces truth-seeking with image management

## Distinction
- **Effort** is not verification.
- **Questioning** is not curiosity if the answer is already decided.
- **Completeness** is not integrity if the method was invalid.

## Rule for Reviewers
Trust the method, not the costume.
