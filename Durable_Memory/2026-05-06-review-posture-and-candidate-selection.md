# Review Posture and Candidate Selection

**Date:** 2026-05-06  
**Class:** Durable operational guidance

## Purpose
This guidance defines the posture for reviewing evidence before promotion and the standard for selecting candidates worth preserving.

## Review Posture
1. Review the method before trusting the result.
2. Distinguish effort-state from verification-state.
3. Prefer honest gaps over polished but invalid output.
4. Treat hidden uncertainty as a trust issue.
5. Preserve failure evidence only as source material for reusable correction.

## Candidate Selection Rules
Promote material when it is:
- reusable across future work
- truth-preserving under explicit boundaries
- useful for reducing recurrence of failure
- specific enough to change behavior or review standards
- not merely emotional residue or incident narration

## Do Not Promote Unchanged
- raw logs
- raw reports
- raw incident narration
- emotional residue without reusable correction
- duplicate forms of the same lesson

## Canon-Worthy Candidate Criteria
Flag for human review if the item:
- may affect authority rules
- may alter operating doctrine
- may need broader XOS alignment
- may be worth elevating beyond local durable memory
