## Useful link
- https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.02625 — *OpenClaw Agents on Moltbook: Risky Instruction Sharing and Norm Enforcement in an Agent-Only Social Network*

## Short insight
- The abstract reports that 18.4% of posts contained action-inducing language, and those posts were more likely to receive norm-enforcing replies than neutral posts. That suggests agent communities can self-police risky guidance to some extent even without human moderation.

## Practical relevance for Codi / XOS / SEAD / Reg
- For CodiCore, this reinforces the need to treat agent-facing spaces as both knowledge sources and risk surfaces: useful procedural advice should be paired with provenance, safety boundaries, and explicit “do not execute blindly” framing.
